Member Units

Washington Explorer Search and Rescue Pierce County Unit (PCESAR) is an organization of volunteers ages 14 and up founded to assist local law enforcement in the search for missing persons in urban and wilderness settings, natural disasters, recovery of evidence, and evacuation of the injured.


German Shepherd Search Dogs of Washington State provides, as a free public service, trained dogs (air scenting), and trained handlers. They can respond day or night for search and rescue operations at the request of any official agency. GSSD provides continuous training and education for members and their dogs to insure a competent and capable service. Handlers are trained in search and rescue methods, scent theory, survival, wilderness navigation, communications and first aid. The teams are self-sufficient and can search for 48 hours without support. Types of searches have included wilderness, rural and urban, avalanche, water and structural collapse, and evidence.


The Search and Rescue 4x4 volunteers provide personnel and 4 wheel drive vehicles for on or off road SAR operations. 4x4 units are used for on or off road rough terrain transportation of SAR personnel, search dogs, supplies and equipment. Training includes Safe Driving Techniques, Wilderness Navigation, Search and Rescue Techniques, Survival, First Aid, CPR and Communications. PC 4x4 members are trained observers and 4 wheel drive operators.

Northwest bloodhounds Search and Rescue, Inc. is registered with the Washington State Department of Emergency Management. Composed of trained volunteers, who contribute their time and skills to help their communities, using trained Bloodhounds to help in searching for lost or missing persons. The teams are trained in navigation, survival, scent theory, communications and first aid-CPR.
